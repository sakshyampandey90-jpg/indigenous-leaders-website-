Which Indigenous Leader's Values Do You Connect With?

This website helps students learn about Indigenous leaders, communities, achievements, and allyship. The goal is not to “become” Indigenous or claim an identity. The goal is to discover which leader’s values connect with your own and learn how their work challenges stereotypes.

Take the Quiz

Inquiry Question

How can learning about Indigenous leaders and communities help students challenge stereotypes and become better allies?

Why This Matters

Many students learn about Indigenous peoples mainly through hardship, residential schools, or stereotypes. Those topics are important, but allyship also means learning about Indigenous success, leadership, knowledge, creativity, and resistance.

TRC Connection

This project connects to the Truth and Reconciliation Commission’s Calls to Action 62–65, which focus on education for reconciliation. My website supports this by helping students learn about Indigenous historical and contemporary contributions.

How This Website Encourages Allyship

  • It teaches students about Indigenous leaders instead of only stereotypes.
  • It shows that Indigenous communities are diverse and have many forms of leadership.
  • It encourages students to connect values like justice, education, water protection, and storytelling to real people.
  • It reminds students that allyship begins with listening, learning, and supporting Indigenous voices.
